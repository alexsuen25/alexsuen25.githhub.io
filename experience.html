Experience

Experience

During my time in high school, I have gained work experience through working at the Wave. At the Wave, I worked as a recreation aide. I helped recreation leaders in offering a range of recreation programs pertaining to aquatics, preschool, playground, after school programs, parks, teenagers, sports, special events, and senior citizen activities under general supervision. From this experience, I gained communication and teamwork skills that allowed me to further expand my skill set. This can help in the future when I am dealing with customers and having to solve problems on my own. I will have the confidence and experience to deal with future issues since I worked at the Wave before.  

An influential speaker that I was able to listen in on was one from Walmart's AI department. During her talk, she explained the ideals of AI and how different parts work together to function seamlessly on the consumer end. One example she gave was the sorting algorithms and AI implementations when deciding what products to show to the consumer first. Something that I learned was the amount of GPU power that is required to making AI work faster and quicker along with how teams decided what information was necessary to collect to power their AI algorithm.  They needed to decide what to collect because collecting too much information would make the user experience slower and less pleasant. 

Since the summer of 2022, I have been researching silicon-based anodes for lithium-ion batteries and finding the optimal particle size that would maximize the energy capacity along with keeping a sustainable cycle life. Along with other high-schoolers, we perform electrochemical experiments such as cyclic voltammetry and galvanostatic charge-discharge to determine traits such as specific capacity and charge retention. Our results were promising and we were able to present our research at two international conferences.
